# **Section 3 — Pre-Proposal Process**

Two research steps happen between triage and writing. Skipping them produces generic proposals that get ignored.

## **3.1 90-Second Client Investigation**

Once a job passes triage, spend 90 seconds reverse-engineering the client before writing a single word.

| **What to Find**              | **Where to Look**                                                                                         |
| ----------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Client's first name           | Past job reviews — previous freelancers often address the client by name in their public review responses |
| Company name or website       | Job description, client profile, past job postings                                                        |
| Existing marketing/data stack | Job description mentions of tools, past job postings, company website                                     |
| Exact symptom language        | The specific words and phrases the client used to describe their problem — mirror these in your hook      |

## **3.2 Loom Video — Strategic Deployment**

A custom Loom video is the single most potent trust signal for a zero-review profile. It is not a template to drop into every proposal — it is a weapon deployed selectively.

| **Record a custom Loom when:**                                          | **Skip the Loom when:**                                                  |
| ----------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Client included their website URL (screen-record their actual setup)    | Budget is at the floor ($30-$50) — client unlikely to watch              |
| Budget is meaningfully above floor — signals serious buyer              | No website or assets provided — nothing concrete to reference            |
| Zero-review context — custom video compensates for missing social proof | Education category jobs — data shows Loom hurts reply rates in Education |

**Loom Video Rules**

- 60-90 seconds maximum — high definition, clear audio, clean background, captions added

- SCARA structure: Hook (10s, state the business problem) → Action (30s, show the fix visually) → Result (10s, quantifiable outcome) → Next Step (10s, soft CTA)

- Custom to the client — reference their specific platform, symptom, or URL. A generic video works against you

- Link goes in the first two sentences of the proposal — if it falls below the 'read more' fold, clients will not see it

- If in Design/Video/Animation category: use Vimeo, not Loom — Vimeo plays natively in Upwork's proposal preview

# **Section 5 — Pricing \*\***&\***\* Bidding Strategy**

## **5.1 The Bidding U-Curve**

Analysis of 60,000 proposals shows that bidding at the posted budget is the single worst strategy. The reply rate follows a U-curve: both extremes win, the safe middle loses.

| **Bid Position**                    | **Reply Rate**     | **Signal to Client**                                              |
| ----------------------------------- | ------------------ | ----------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Under 50% of posted budget          | **20.6% — BEST**   | Low-friction, fast execution option                               |
| 200-500% over posted budget         | **16.4% — STRONG** | Specialist premium, forces them to read your proposal             |
| 95-105% of posted budget (matching) | **8.8% — WORST**   | Generic template signal — clients see budget-matching as laziness |
| 1.0-1.2x client's average paid rate | **5.4% — AVOID**   | Marginally expensive without proof of premium value               |

| **RULE: Never bid 95-105% of the posted budget. Bid under 50% to offer a low-friction entry point, or 200-500% over to signal specialist authority. Never in the safe middle.** |

## **5.4 Scope Protection on Fixed-Price Jobs**

# **Section 6 — Project Delivery**

## **6.1 Pre-Work Checklist**

- Confirm you have all required access (GTM Editor, GA4 Admin, site CMS access) before starting

- Screenshot the current state before making any changes — proof of 'before' for your delivery report

- Identify any existing conflicts (hardcoded GA4 snippets, duplicate plugins, conflicting analytics tools)

- Confirm scope in writing with the client before beginning

## **6.2 Phase 1 Job Delivery Standards**

| **Deliverable**     | **Standard**                                                                                                                                                                       |
| ------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| GTM Installation    | Container live, GA4 Config tag firing on all pages, Enhanced Measurement configured, verification screenshots (GTM Preview + GA4 DebugView)                                        |
| GA4 Setup / Fix     | Property configured correctly, events firing with correct parameters, no duplicate tracking sources, data retention set to 14 months, internal traffic filter Active (not Testing) |
| Tag / Trigger Fix   | Root cause identified, fix applied, before/after screenshots, plain-English explanation of what caused it                                                                          |
| Audit               | Written report with CRITICAL / SHOULD FIX / NICE TO HAVE / WORKING CORRECTLY sections, prioritised fix list                                                                        |
| Conversion Tracking | Conversion actions verified in Google Ads, purchase/lead events confirmed end-to-end, Loom walkthrough of the working setup                                                        |

## **6.3 Key Technical Rules**

- GA4 Config tag trigger: always Initialization — All Pages. Never Page View.

- GTM Google Tag (AW-XXXXXXXXX full format) vs Conversion Tracking tag (numerals only) — sharing one constant variable causes double-prefixing and silent failure

- WooCommerce purchase event: test with a fresh order — GTM4WP's order-already-tracked flag silently skips repeat visits to the same order URL

- Shopify non-Plus: GTM via theme.liquid does not fire on hosted checkout pages — requires Customer Pixels + Measurement Protocol for checkout events

- GA4 Realtime (not DebugView) is the correct Shopify verification method for checkout events

- CF7: use wpcf7mailsent / wpcf7mailfailed / wpcf7invalid (three separate listeners), not a single wpcf7submit handler

# **Section 8 — JSS Protection**

## **8.1 How JSS Works (2026 Update)**

| **Factor**             | **Detail**                                                                                                                                                                                                          |
| ---------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Calculation frequency  | Daily, across 6-month, 12-month, and 24-month rolling windows — your profile shows the highest (best) score                                                                                                         |
| Private feedback       | REMOVED as of March 31, 2026. Only the public 1-5 star rating now feeds JSS. Any private feedback received before March 2026 still affects your score until those contracts age out of the 24-month window.         |
| Contract weighting     | Higher-value contracts ($1,000+) carry a 1.5x weight multiplier vs under $250 (1.0x). A high-paying contract amplifies both positive and negative outcomes.                                                         |
| Long-term contracts    | Contracts with active payments extending past 90 days are automatically counted as successful outcomes, even without feedback. Every 90 days of continuous billing = one extra positive job (max weight of 8 jobs). |
| Zero-earning contracts | Canceled with no earnings and no feedback = excluded from JSS (not harmful). Canceled with no earnings but negative feedback = severe JSS penalty.                                                                  |

## **8.2 The Refund Trap**

| A full refund removes the client's public written review from your visible profile, but the underlying negative rating data and contract failure markers remain permanently in Upwork's JSS database. Refunds are NOT a clean escape from a bad outcome — they still damage your score. If a project is going sideways, it is better to offer a partial refund, complete the current milestone, and close the contract cleanly rather than refunding and canceling. |
| ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |

## **8.3 Risk at Zero Reviews**

The math is brutally asymmetric at the start. Upwork requires at least two contracts with two different clients before generating your first JSS. If you only have one or two completed contracts and one results in a dispute or negative feedback, your very first JSS could debut below 60% — the 'Complete Suppression' tier where your profile is excluded from active search queues.

- Only take jobs where client satisfaction is 100% predictable — highly defined scope, clear deliverable

- Never start a large ambiguous fixed-price project as your first jobs

- If a project is going wrong, communicate immediately — clients rate cooperation highly

- Confirm client satisfaction before asking them to close the contract

# **Section 9 — Review Generation**

## **9.1 The Handoff Ritual**

The handoff is your best opportunity to generate a 5-star review. Clients leave reviews based on how the project ended, not just the quality of the work.

- Deliver a clear, organised delivery report (see Section 6)

- Send a Loom walkthrough of the working implementation — seeing you explain it builds final confidence

- Explicitly confirm everything is working before closing

- Ask if they have any questions before you wrap up

## **9.2 Contract Close Message**

| Hi [Name], Everything is working correctly — [one line summary of what was delivered and verified]. The [deliverable] is live and confirmed in [GTM Preview / GA4 DebugView / Google Ads]. If you have any questions after you've had a chance to review, I'm happy to help. If everything looks good on your end, would you be able to close it out when you have a chance? It helps me keep my stats accurate on Upwork. [Your name] |
| -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |

Do not mention the review explicitly. The act of closing the contract is what triggers Upwork to prompt the client for a review.

## **9.3 Responding to Reviews**

| **Review type** | **Response approach**                                                                                                                                                                           |
| --------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 5-star          | 'Thank you — it was a pleasure working on this. Happy to help with any future GA4 or GTM work whenever it comes up.'                                                                            |
| 4-star          | 'Thank you for the feedback. I'm glad the [deliverable] is working correctly. I'll keep your notes in mind for future projects.'                                                                |
| 3-star or below | 'Thank you for the honest feedback. I'm sorry the result didn't fully meet your expectations — I'm reviewing what could have been done differently and will apply those lessons going forward.' |

Never argue with a review publicly. A gracious response to a negative review is more impressive to future clients than a string of 5-star responses.

## **11.4 Phase 2 Pricing Strategy**

Phase 2 moves progressively away from time-based pricing toward value-based pricing, where contract value is anchored to the financial ROI you generate for the client rather than hours worked.

- Start Phase 2 with fixed-price project-based billing — clear scope, defined deliverable

- Introduce retainer conversations after a second or third project with the same client ('I can be your ongoing tracking resource at X/month for Y hours')

- Anchor proposals to client business outcomes: 'This server-side setup will recover approximately X% of your lost iOS attribution data'

- Raise rates 15-25% every 90 days when: JSS 95%+, 5+ category reviews, 20%+ proposal-to-interview conversion, actively turning away work

- Do not raise rates incrementally week-to-week — raise in firm, milestone-driven steps

## **11.5 Planting Phase 2 Seeds During Phase 1**

Use every Phase 1 engagement to create the conditions for a Phase 2 conversation without over-promising.

- After every GTM installation: identify the question 'which channels are driving conversions?' and mention it in your delivery report as an observation, not a pitch

- After every tag fix: note setup quality issues beyond the scope of the fix — 'Your tracking is now working correctly. One thing I noticed is that your GA4 data retention is set to 2 months — setting it to 14 months would preserve more historical data for analysis'

- Every audit job is a Phase 2 opportunity — an audit that finds a reconciliation gap between GA4 revenue and actual backend revenue is a direct introduction to attribution work

- Build server-side GTM and CAPI skills in parallel to your Phase 1 delivery — your positioning should lag your actual capability by one phase
